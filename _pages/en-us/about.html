About

I'm a psychologist who's spent years in the space between people and technology, and what I keep coming back to is pretty simple: I want to understand people. What people want and need, how they connect with each other, and how all of that shifts over time. Lately a lot of my work is about how people relate to AI chatbots, and what that's doing to the ways we interact with one another.

My path started with kinesiology at Queen's, came across a wearable EEG club and got interested in the brain, and went on to do my M.A. and PhD at the University of Toronto. I've spent time in a few labs along the way (the Stokes Lab at Johns Hopkins with Prof. Wang Jian, Randy McIntosh's Computational Neuroscience Lab, and Cendri Hutcherson's Decision Neuroscience Lab), and these days I'm in Liz Page-Gould's SPRQL Lab, looking at how people's social worlds get built and how well they hold up.

People aren't fixed. What I love about studying people and social groups is catching them in motion, both the slow drift over months and the small shifts in a single interaction, and making sense of why each person said and did what they did in that moment.
