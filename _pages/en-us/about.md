I'm a psychologist with a foot firmly in the tech world. My research sits at the intersection of human behavior, consciousness, and AI.

My academic path started with a B.S. in Kinesiology at Queen's University, where I learned how the body and the brain works. Then I focused on human behavior and social network during my M.A. and Ph.D. at the University of Toronto. Along the way I've researched at the Stroke Lab at Johns Hopkins advised by Prof. Wang Jian, the McIntosh Lab with Prof. Randy McIntosh, and the Decision Neuroscience Lab with Prof. Cendri Hutcherson.

When I'm not running studies or building tech projects, you'll find me on the golf course, fishing for bass around the GTA, or playing volleyball on a beach somewhere. Want to get in touch? Email me at leo.huang4977@gmail.com.
